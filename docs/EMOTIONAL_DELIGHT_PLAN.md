# Caelyn — Emotional Delight & Celebration Plan (2026-07)

4-lens study (motion designer, warm companion writer, womanly-aesthetic director, guardrail critic) + synthesis. PLAN ONLY.

## Philosophy

Delight is a response to what she DID, never decoration sprayed at her — every celebration must be earned by a real action or milestone, physically subtle (spring, not cartoon), and carried by Caelyn's compassionate copy rather than graphics. That is what keeps "cute + cheering" dignified for Ruth (47) while still delighting Maya (15): the same phase-tinted "the app heard you" beat reads as premium responsiveness to one and as being listened-to by the other. We hold the line with three rules the guardrail critic and every other lens agreed on — no emoji in the visual language (SF Rounded + the cycle ring ARE the character), no infinite animation (finite heartbeats only, reduceMotion-safe), and no imperatives/medical advice in copy (only observations of her data and her self-knowledge). Warmth is opt-in, never age-gated, because the app promises it never asks her age. If a moment can't be tied to her body's data or a completed action, it doesn't ship.

## The signature celebration (one motif, reused everywhere)

The phase-tinted "Caelyn heard you" bloom, tied to the cycle ring's own color language (menstrual = softRose, ovulation = sage, PMS = lavender, follicular/luteal = warmSand). ONE motif reused everywhere: (1) on every log chip/pill it is a spring settle (0.94→1.0, response 0.3, damping 0.6) plus a ~220ms easeOut color-fill into the CURRENT PHASE's tint instead of the flat plum/lavender it uses today; (2) on every one-time milestone card it is that same phase tint as the card background (replacing the hardcoded lavender) with a single 0.3s opacity fade-in on appear, then static. Same physics, same palette, same restraint from the smallest tap to the biggest milestone — so the whole app feels like one instrument, and the ring's colors become the language of every celebration. Fully reduceMotion-safe (static fill/instant appear when reduced). This is already ~80% built on MoodChip/SymptomChip and in the celebrationCard helper; the work is unifying the tint, not inventing new motion.
