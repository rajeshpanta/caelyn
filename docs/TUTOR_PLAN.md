# Caelyn — Tutor/Guide Layer: Execution Plan (2026-07)

Produced by a 3-design × 4-critic workflow (senior iOS dev, 63-year-old non-tech user, 15-year-old
first-period user, simplicity czar) + synthesis. STATUS: PLAN ONLY — nothing built yet.

## Is this overkill?

No — as scoped here it is not overkill, because it adds zero new navigation: it upgrades one existing line on Home (the phase hint) and fills an existing sheet (the phase-badge guide) that today shows only generic content, at roughly 600–900 lines of mostly authored copy and pure, testable functions. All four critics converged that the danger was never the four gaps themselves — teen-user: "NOT overkill in feature breadth" — but fragmentation and drift: the simplicity czar and older-user both flagged invisible-tutor's five scattered surfaces as the real overkill, and senior-dev flagged ask-first's 1,700-LOC conversational stack as overkill for the same outcome. It BECOMES overkill under four conditions: if teaching text appears on more than the two surfaces named here (Home hint + guide sheet), if free-form AI chat ships, if milestones exceed two or gain animation, or if a second entry point or tab appears. Hold the boundary at the sheet and this is the minimum viable version of "a tutor who teaches her her own body" — anything less fails G1–G4, anything more fails Ruth.

## Chosen approach

Hybrid: companion-page's consolidation as the skeleton, built inside an EXISTING surface. Companion-page won the convergence test — ranked #1 by both the older-user and the simplicity czar, #2 by senior-dev and teen-user, and the only design never ranked last. Its two flagged weaknesses (senior-dev: 'new surface, scope creep, effort underestimated'; older-user: 'discovery relies on her tapping the phase badge') dissolve because we do not build a new page: the phase badge on HomeHeroCard.swift already opens PhaseGuideView.swift as a .large sheet, so the four companion sections (Today for you / Is this normal? / Common questions / What Caelyn learned) fill a sheet users already open, keeping net new user-facing surfaces at ZERO. Two transplants that multiple critics independently endorsed are grafted in: (1) invisible-tutor's personalized daily phase hint on HomeHeroCard — the czar's sole KEEP from that design, teen-user's 'warmest 2am answer', and the heart of senior-dev's top pick — implemented per the czar's fix as an extension of CycleSummaryService, not a new BodyInsightHint service; (2) ask-first's curated question chips — czar: 'KEEP for Companion's AskCaelyView', teen-user's #1 reason, senior-dev: 'chip curation logic is solid' — but deterministic-only, with free-form AI input cut per senior-dev's decisive objection (untestable without an iOS 26 device, hallucination liability) which strengthens rather than contradicts the czar. All czar cuts are obeyed: no StatCard range frames in Insights, no chips on PatternInsightsSection, no Home milestone parade, max 2 milestones, one entry point.

## Design principles (the rules that keep it simple)

1. One place to learn, zero new navigation: the tutor lives entirely behind the phase badge the user already taps (HomeHeroCard.swift line 94 → PhaseGuideView sheet). No new tab, button, or onboarding tooltip (simplicity czar + older-user + owner constraint). The badge's existing accessibility hint — 'Opens a guide to what's happening in your cycle right now' — is already the correct promise.
2. Her number first, typical range second, never 'abnormal': every reassurance reads 'Yours: 31 days · Typical: 21–35' with text status ('✓ In a common range'), cited to ACOG-style sources in code comments, and ends with when-to-talk-to-a-provider framing (all four critics; czar's G1 must-have; teen's 'never abnormal').
3. One source of truth per cycle day: a single CycleSummaryService.dailyTeaching(facts:) feeds BOTH the Home hint and the guide sheet's lesson — no parallel BodyInsightHint service, no duplicated ranges (czar's explicit cut, senior-dev's maintainability point).
4. Deterministic first, AI as garnish: every sentence exists as an authored, vetted template keyed by phase + day window; Foundation Models (iOS 26+) may only rephrase within the existing CycleSummaryService guardrails. The app is fully verifiable in the simulator with AI absent (senior-dev must-have; synthesis rule).
5. Same experience on every iOS version: no iOS-26 UX cliff, no disabled text fields, no 'lesser mode' (older-user must-have G3; czar's cut of ask-first's three UX branches).
6. Milestones are self-knowledge checkpoints, not achievements: exactly two ('Caelyn learned your luteal length', 'Caelyn learned your PMS onset'), text-only, quiet one-time highlight, never animated, never streaked (czar max-2 rule; older-user 'not achievement-y'; teen 'proud facts, not pressure').
7. Reassurance in ≤3 taps at 2am: open app (1) → tap phase badge (2) → tap the question chip that already matches today (3), answered from HER logged data (teen-user must-have).
8. Cold-start grace: at 0 cycles everything still renders — typical ranges with 'your number appears after your first cycle', supportive generic hint — nothing looks locked or broken (senior-dev must-have).
9. Closed scope: the guide sheet is the boundary. Future teaching ideas go inside it or don't ship; nothing gets bolted onto Insights cards, stat grids, or Home (teen-user's product-drift warning; czar's anti-fragmentation verdict).

## First-period mode

A single free toggle ('First-period mode — gentler guidance for the first years of periods') on UserProfile + CycleSettingsView, offered where cycle length is set — no separate UI, no new screens, no age collection (privacy rule: we never ask her age, she simply opts in). It is purely a copy-and-ranges variant flowing through the same three catalogs: (1) TypicalRanges switches to adolescent framing — cycles of 21–45 days shown as typical, with the reassurance that irregular and even skipped cycles are very common in the first 1–2 years after a first period, so a scary-looking 40-day gap reads '✓ Common in the first years' instead of borderline; (2) dailyTeaching uses gentler, shorter strings — 'Day 2 — cramps and tiredness are common. A heating pad and rest genuinely help. You're doing a good job learning your body' — with no hormone jargon unless she opens the guide; (3) GuideQuestions reorders chips to first-period basics answered from vetted templates: 'How long will my period last?' ('Most last 3–7 days. Yours have averaged 5 so far — right in the common range'), 'Why do I get cramps?', 'Is it normal to skip a month?' ('Very — especially in the first year or two. If you skip three or more in a row, mention it to a doctor or school nurse'). Milestones keep the same two-checkpoint structure but soften: 'You've tracked your first full cycle — that's a real skill most people never learn.' The pre-existing warm fallback line for 0 cycles doubles as her day-1 experience, so Maya at 2am on her first period gets reassurance in the same ≤3 taps as everyone else — same surfaces, same privacy, gentler voice.

## Deliberately NOT building

1. A new 'Ask Caelyn' sheet, page, or tab (ask-first's AskCaelynSheet + 5 companion views, ~1,700 LOC) — senior-dev's overkill verdict and the czar's #3 ranking of scattered/new surfaces; the existing phase-badge sheet already owns this job at zero navigation cost.
2. Free-form 'ask me anything' AI text input — senior-dev's decisive cut: untestable without an iOS 26 device, hallucination/medical-liability exposure, and it creates the three-branch UX cliff the older-user critic rejected (iOS <26 users must get the SAME experience, not a downgraded picker). Curated chips answer the same questions safely on every device. Revisit only after Foundation Models is stable and a red-team pass exists.
3. Typical-range frames on the InsightsStatsGrid StatCards — czar's explicit cut (duplication with the guide panel) and the older-user must-have that G1 reassurance be consolidated in ONE revisitable place. StatCard's existing hint parameter stays as-is.
4. Q&A chips appended to PatternInsightsSection cards — czar's cut ('too scattered'); teen-user also asked to deprioritize this layer to reduce cognitive load.
5. The Home milestone card parade (invisible-tutor's 4 AppStorage cards with a per-session queue) — czar cut it as unpredictable ('why did 2 cards appear today?'), older-user found even quiet cards achievement-y, senior-dev flagged the week-1 pileup. Milestones live as two quiet learned-rows inside the sheet.
6. Milestones #3 and #4 ('20 days logged', 'confidence upgraded') — czar's max-2 rule; the existing streak card and the hero card's 'log a few more cycles and predictions will improve' line already cover encouragement without new machinery.
7. A standalone BodyInsightHint service — czar's cut; folding daily teaching into CycleSummaryService keeps one source of truth per cycle day and reuses its proven AI-guard/fallback shape.
8. A second entry point from Insights — czar: one entry (Home phase badge). Insights already has its own CycleSummaryCard and LearnedAboutYouSection; adding a door there splits discovery.
9. Green/yellow/red status dots — companion-page's own accessibility risk (colorblind/VoiceOver); text status only ('✓ In a common range' / 'worth mentioning to your doctor').
10. Any gamification: streaks framing on milestones, entrance animations, confetti, badges, counts — unanimous across all four critics.
11. Launch-time i18n of the copy catalogs, CloudKit sync of seen/dismissed state, or any question-usage analytics — out of MVP scope and the last two violate the no-server privacy brand rules.


## Day in the life (before/after: Ruth 63 & Maya 15)

1. DAY 1 — BEFORE: Ruth (63, tracking irregular cycles ahead of a doctor visit) opens Home and sees 'Day 1' with the static hint 'Take it easy today.' Insights is mostly dashes. Her real question — 'my cycles jumped from 26 to 40 days, is that alarming?' — has no home in the app; she googles it and lands on forums. Maya (15, first period ever) logs her flow, sees the same generic hint, and at 2am searches 'is it normal for a first period to hurt' on TikTok.
2. DAY 1 — AFTER: Home looks IDENTICAL — with <1 cycle of data the hint stays the familiar phase.hint, so nothing new to decode (cold-start grace). But the phase badge Ruth was already curious about now opens a guide whose 'Is this normal?' panel shows typical ranges with 'Your number will appear after your first logged cycle' — she learns 21–35 days is typical before Caelyn knows anything about her, and the footer tells her exactly what's worth raising with her doctor. Maya (first-period mode on) taps the same badge; the first chip is 'How long will my period last?' → 'Most last 3–7 days. The first ones are often irregular — that's very normal.' Two taps, on-device, 2am, no forum.
3. DAY 22 (PMS window) — BEFORE: Home says 'Be gentle with yourself' — kind but generic; it said the same thing last month. Ruth feels an unusual mood dip and can't tell whether it's her cycle or her age. Maya feels exhausted and wonders if something is wrong with her; the static luteal guide talks about 'the corpus luteum' but not about HER.
4. DAY 22 — AFTER: Ruth's Home hint now reads 'Day 22 — progesterone is peaking. You've logged low energy here in 4 of 5 cycles. That's your pattern, not a flaw.' One glance, zero taps, her own data. She taps the badge: 'Is this normal?' shows 'Cycle length — Yours: 24 days · Typical: 21–35 · ✓ In a common range', and the luteal-first chip 'Why is my mood lower right now?' answers from her logs and ends with 'if this regularly gets in the way of your life, a doctor can help — ask about PMDD screening.' Maya's gentler hint says tiredness before a period is her body's chemistry, not laziness — reassurance in ≤3 taps for both, identical on Ruth's older iPhone (no AI) and Maya's new one (AI politely rephrases the same vetted facts).
5. MONTH 4 — BEFORE: Insights shows numbers ('avg cycle 31') and 'Still learning' rows, but nothing marks the moment Caelyn actually figured her out; the growing intelligence is invisible, so both users still feel like they're feeding a spreadsheet.
6. MONTH 4 — AFTER: Ruth opens the guide and the learned row has quietly flipped with a one-time sage highlight: '✓ Learned: your luteal phase is 13 days — your ovulation estimate now uses it.' No confetti, no badge — a checkpoint of self-knowledge. Her Home hint now says 'your PMS usually starts ~3 days out — likely tomorrow,' and she screenshots the Is-this-normal panel for her doctor's appointment: her numbers, typical ranges, framed observationally. Maya's second milestone reads 'You've tracked your first full cycles — a real skill most people never learn,' and her chips have graduated from first-period basics to her own emerging patterns. Neither woman learned a new interface in four months; the two surfaces they already used simply got wiser about them.
